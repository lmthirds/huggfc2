Poetry Flying Over the Battlefield

When steel remembers only conflict, words become wings. Every verse crosses the smoke, searching not for victory, but for humanity.

Above the fields where thunder spoke, A quiet poem learned to fly. It carried no sword, No banner, No command. Only lines woven From hope, Memory, And forgotten names. The wind lifted every syllable Beyond the reach of cannons. Bullets pierced the air, Yet never touched The invisible feathers Of a single verse. A child looked upward. A soldier lowered his rifle. The silence between explosions Became another stanza. The sky gathered every broken dream And folded it into light. Across the battlefield, Poetry continued its flight— Not to conquer, But to remember That every heartbeat Deserves another morning.
"Where words survive, humanity still breathes."
